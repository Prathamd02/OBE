Outcome-Based Education (OBE) focuses on what students should achieve by the end of a course, emphasizing skills and real-world application. It uses clearly defined learning outcomes to design teaching methods and assessments. OBE ensures continuous improvement and prepares students for career success.
Traditional education focuses on content delivery, while OBE emphasizes achieving measurable learning outcomes by aligning assessments and teaching methods with specific objectives.
CO-PO Mapping is the process of linking Course Outcomes (COs) with Program Outcomes (POs) to ensure that the courses contribute to achieving the overall program objectives. It helps in assessing whether students are acquiring the necessary knowledge and skills as per the curriculum.
